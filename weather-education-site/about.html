About the project

About WeatherLab

WeatherLab is a weather education website and DIY build project designed to explain weather in a simple, hands-on way.

The idea

Learn weather by connecting it to real life.

WeatherLab is being built to help people understand weather through real examples, simple explanations, interactive tools, and a DIY home weather station.

The goal is to make weather easier to understand for pilots, boaters, storm chasers, makers, and anyone who wants to know what the atmosphere is doing.

Main focus areas

Aviation Weather Marine Weather Storm Chasing DIY Weather Station Weather Tools

Why this exists

Weather can be confusing, but it does not have to be.

Many weather websites show numbers, maps, and forecasts, but they do not always explain what those numbers mean or why they matter. WeatherLab is designed to fill that gap.

Simple Explanations

Weather concepts will be explained in plain English without making the topic feel overwhelming.

Interactive Tools

The Weather Lab will include tools that show the math behind clouds, dew point, altitude temperature, wind, and more.

Hands-On Building

The DIY weather station project will use sensors, code, and 3D printed parts to explain how weather data is measured.

Important note

Educational use only

WeatherLab is designed for education and learning. The tools and lessons on this site should not replace official weather briefings, forecasts, warnings, or safety information.

For real-world aviation, marine, storm chasing, or emergency decisions, always use official FAA, NWS, NOAA, and local emergency sources.
